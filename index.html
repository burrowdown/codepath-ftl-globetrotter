About Hells Canyon

Hells Canyon is the deepest river gorge in North America, carved by the Snake River along the border of Oregon and Idaho. Plunging over 7,900 feet from the peaks of the Seven Devils Mountains to the river below, it surpasses even the Grand Canyon in depth. The canyon stretches roughly 125 miles and is largely roadless, making it a haven for wilderness adventurers. Visitors come for whitewater rafting, hiking, fishing, and wildlife watching — with opportunities to spot bighorn sheep, black bears, mountain goats, and golden eagles. The Nez Perce people called this rugged landscape home for thousands of years, and evidence of their presence can still be found along the canyon walls today.
