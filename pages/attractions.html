At 8,429 feet, Heavens Gate Lookout offers one of the most dramatic views in the entire canyon system. On a clear day you can see into four states — Idaho, Oregon, Washington, and Montana. The lookout sits within the Hells Canyon Wilderness and is reached by a short trail from the Seven Devils Campground. Kirkwood Ranch is a historic homestead tucked along the Snake River, accessible only by boat or trail. The ranch dates to the early 1900s and is now maintained as a living museum by the Forest Service, with original buildings, orchards, and outbuildings still intact. Jet boat tours regularly stop here, making it one of the few inner-canyon destinations reachable without a multiday commitment. Hells Canyon is home to one of the most diverse wildlife populations in the Pacific Northwest. Rocky outcrops shelter bighorn sheep and mountain goats, while black bears roam the forested upper slopes. Golden eagles and peregrine falcons nest along the canyon walls, and the river corridor supports one of the last wild populations of white sturgeon in North America. Cache Creek Ranch is the Forest Service's primary administrative site inside Hells Canyon. The historic homestead is reachable only by boat or trail through the wilderness. The ranch sits at the mouth of Cache Creek and preserves the remnants of early canyon settlement, including original structures and terraced gardens carved into the steep terrain. It's a quiet stop on jet boat tours and a rewarding destination for river travelers looking to connect with the canyon's human history, or obtain boat or fishing permits. Hells Canyon is renowned for world-class fishing, with the Snake River supporting robust populations of steelhead, chinook salmon, smallmouth bass, and white sturgeon — one of North America's largest freshwater fish. The remote stretches of the inner canyon are accessible only by boat, giving anglers access to water that sees relatively little pressure. Both guided and self-guided trips are available out of Lewiston, Idaho and Hells Canyon Dam.
